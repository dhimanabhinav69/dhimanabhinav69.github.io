slide 03 / business problem

The stakeholder problem

Sales performance was spread across multiple dimensions: products, customers, geography, dates, and order channels. Without a structured dashboard, a stakeholder would spend too much time switching between raw tables and manually comparing slices of performance.

  • Which product categories are contributing most to sales?
  • Which territories show growth, stagnation, or underperformance?
  • How are customers behaving across order volume and average order value?
  • Which monthly patterns should sales leadership prepare for?
  • Where should the business focus next: acquisition, product mix, or regional execution?

slide 04 / analytical approach

A clean pipeline from raw data to decision-ready insight.

01

Understand

Defined the core business questions before choosing charts, so the dashboard would answer decision needs instead of just displaying data.

02

Model

Structured sales, product, customer, territory, and date tables into a simple star-schema style reporting model.

03

Measure

Built KPI logic for revenue, orders, average order value, customer count, growth, and category contribution.

04

Visualise

Created executive, product, region, and customer views with filters for time period and category-level exploration.

05

Recommend

Converted dashboard patterns into business actions around product prioritisation, territory focus, and sales planning.

slide 05 / insights

What the dashboard is built to reveal.

01

Category dependency

The dashboard separates high-revenue categories from smaller add-on categories, making it easier to see whether sales growth is broad-based or overly dependent on one product group.

02

Regional execution gaps

Territory views help identify markets that are growing, declining, or underpenetrated, so leadership can decide where to intensify sales efforts.

03

Customer value patterns

Customer and order metrics highlight whether revenue is driven by more buyers, larger orders, or repeat demand from existing customers.

slide 06 / recommendations

Business actions suggested by the analysis.

Product mix

Protect the strongest sales categories while building cross-sell bundles around accessories and complementary products.

Territory focus

Use regional growth and order-volume differences to prioritise field-sales support, campaign spend, and inventory planning.

Forecasting rhythm

Review sales trend views monthly so seasonality and demand shifts are caught before planning cycles become reactive.

Dashboard adoption

Keep the executive overview simple, then allow deeper drilldowns for analysts who need customer, product, or territory detail.
