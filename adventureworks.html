slide 04 / business problem

The stakeholder problem

AdventureWorks performance sits across product, customer, geography, calendar, sales, and returns tables. Without a structured report, stakeholders would need to manually compare separate views to understand growth, product demand, customer quality, regional concentration, and operational issues.

  • Which product categories are generating the most order volume?
  • Which countries and regions are contributing most to total orders?
  • Which products are meeting or missing order, revenue, and profit targets?
  • Which customers contribute the most orders and revenue?
  • Where should leadership focus next: product mix, territory execution, customer value, or returns reduction?

slide 06 / data model

Data model and report logic

The model uses fact-style sales and returns data with supporting lookup tables for calendar, customer, product, category, territory, and measures.

Sales DataOrder quantity, revenue, product keys, customer keys, and transactional performance.
Returns DataReturn volume and return-rate logic for product and category monitoring.
Product LookupProduct names, subcategories, categories, and drill-through selection.
Customer LookupCustomer profile, occupation, gender, order behaviour, and revenue contribution.
Territory LookupCountry and region fields supporting the map and regional slicer buttons.
Calendar LookupWeekly and monthly trend analysis across 2020, 2021, and 2022.
Measure TableCentralized DAX KPIs for revenue, profit, orders, returns, targets, and selected metrics.

slide 07 / insights

What the dashboard is built to reveal.

01

Revenue momentum

The weekly revenue view highlights growth direction and makes it easy to see when sales acceleration becomes visible.

02

Product dependency

Category and top-product views separate high-volume product groups from smaller categories, helping leadership understand demand concentration.

03

Regional concentration

The country map makes order concentration visible so regional campaigns, inventory, and sales planning can be targeted more precisely.

slide 08 / recommendations

Business actions suggested by the analysis.

Product mix

Protect the strongest order categories and build cross-sell bundles around accessories, tires, tubes, helmets, and complementary products.

Territory focus

Use the map view to prioritize sales campaigns, field support, stock allocation, and regional growth planning.

Returns watch

Monitor return rate by product and subcategory to identify potential quality, fulfillment, sizing, or expectation gaps.

Executive rhythm

Review weekly and monthly dashboard pages regularly so the business reacts early to demand shifts instead of waiting for quarter-end reporting.
